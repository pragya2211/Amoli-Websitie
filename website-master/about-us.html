About Us

What?

‘The eyes don’t see what the mind doesn’t know’ Amoli, meaning ‘precious’ is an NGO started by 8 college students, which educates children about the abnormality of Child Sexual Abuse prevailing in the society and helps in protection of their innocence. We aim at making them courageous and strong enough to not only protect themselves from this social evil, but also stand against it if needed. We inculcate in them the thought that it is not their fault and that they should come out strongly against the wrong. We ensure that the children do not engage in self-shame, self-blame and self-harm.

‘I didn’t know I was sexually abused until I found out what it was’

At present, Amoli is focussing on spreading awareness among government school children where there is lack of education among the masses. Generally, the children here belong to impoverished families where parents go paranoid and awkward while communicating about CSA openly. Thus, we step in to edify these children about CSA using inquisitive techniques in our sessions.

Why?

‘I would trade anything in the world to get those few minutes of my childhood back.’ With hopeful hearts and minds, yet to be nurtured with the sense of fair and foul, the little kids come up with such heart wrenching statements that it has become a necessity to help them feel safe.

CSA is the erotic use of a child whether physically or emotionally, and their sexual exploitation in the fullest meaning of the term, even if no bodily contact is ever made. According to a survey, the ratio of child victims to adult victims is 3:1. CSA not only demolishes the innocence of childhood, but also scars their adulthood. Studies have observed that most of the times, victims themselves become the abusers.

This makes it important for us to help children differentiate between good and bad touch, and to encourage them to speak boldly without any shame, in the earlier phase itself. The trauma of sexual abuse may heal physically, but emotional trauma endures throughout life, thereby hampering their performance as an individual. This creates a strong urge in us to educate them about it because ‘When you are aware, you dare’.

How?

‘The bruises may have gone away, but the pain remains’ Amoli conducts two sessions per school - one session explaining the basics and the second one in which we dwell into more details. The interactive session is conducted weekly for an hour for children below the age of thirteen. We have covered over 1000 kids so far. We talk about prevention and teach the little kids that they are masters of their own bodies and talking, touching or even looking at their genitals or ‘danger zones’ is wrong. Being little, the kids are prone to get scared and take our teachings in an extreme sense. This is why we play games and tell them stories in order to make them sympathetic towards the topic.

We pledge to eradicate within them senses of self-harm, self-blame and self-shame so that they are filled with courage to talk about the same. We play, act and shout along with them inducing in them a sense of belongingness and connectivity which makes them catch up on the concept fast and steady. Slogans are recited so that each one is imprinted with the fact that ‘I am unique and have full right on my body’.

Our mission and Vision

  • To make world a safer place for children.
  • To make the children aware about safe & bad unsafe while preserving their innocence.

Amoli thrives to educate children about the abnormality of Child Sexual Abuse and to make the world a better place for them. We at Amoli visualise a society in which each and every child is aware about the harsh realities of CSA and have proper knowledge of protecting themselves from it.

We not only impart knowledge but are also all ears if a child wants to share their experience with us. Researches say that 35% of children are victims of sexual abuse. We aim to reduce this number and envision to create a community that understands the rights of children and to provide a “CSA- free” environment to them. By the end of May 2019 we aim to cover more than 50 schools in New Delhi and thus impacting over 6000 students.
